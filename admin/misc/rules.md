# Team Charter and Rules
## Group 09 - Akatsuki

### Purpose
This charter serves as the foundational agreement for Group 09 - Akatsuki. Its purpose is to define how the team will work together throughout the project, establish shared expectations, clarify responsibilities, and promote a respectful, accountable, and collaborative team environment. This document is intended to help the group operate effectively, resolve issues constructively, and maintain trust and clarity throughout the project lifecycle.

---

## 1. Team Purpose and Objectives

Our team’s purpose is to successfully complete the course project through strong collaboration, clear communication, and consistent contributions from all members. We aim to build a project that reflects both technical quality and effective teamwork while creating a productive and supportive working environment.

Our objectives are to:

- Complete all course deliverables on time and to the best of our ability
- Maintain a reliable and organized workflow throughout the quarter
- Ensure that each team member contributes meaningfully to the project
- Communicate openly about progress, concerns, and blockers
- Develop both technical and collaborative skills through the project experience

---

## 2. Common Values and Standards

As a team, we agree to uphold the following values:

- **Respect:** Every team member’s ideas, time, and effort will be treated with respect
- **Accountability:** Each member is responsible for completing assigned work and communicating honestly about progress
- **Transparency:** We will keep one another informed about deadlines, blockers, and availability
- **Collaboration:** We value teamwork, mutual support, and helping one another succeed
- **Reliability:** We will make a good-faith effort to attend meetings, respond to messages, and follow through on responsibilities
- **Quality:** We will aim to produce thoughtful, well-documented, and maintainable work rather than rushing incomplete work
- **Adaptability:** We understand that plans may change, and we will remain flexible and solution-oriented when challenges arise

---

## 3. Personal Goals and Team Goals

Each team member may have individual goals for this project, such as improving technical skills, gaining experience with collaboration tools, building confidence in software development, or learning how to work effectively on a team. We recognize that achieving these personal goals can strengthen the team overall.

As a group, our shared goals are to:

- Learn from the software development process
- Improve our communication and coordination as a team
- Build a project we can be proud of
- Support each other’s growth while meeting all project expectations

---

## 4. Strengths, Weaknesses, and Risk Awareness

Our team recognizes that each member brings different strengths, experiences, and working styles. These differences are valuable and can help us build a stronger final product. At the same time, we acknowledge that challenges may arise, such as scheduling conflicts, communication delays, uneven workloads, or uncertainty around technical tasks.

To address weaknesses and risks, we agree to:

- Ask for help early rather than waiting until deadlines are close
- Be honest about workload, availability, and technical challenges
- Redistribute tasks when necessary to keep the team moving forward
- Support one another in areas where someone may need extra help
- Regularly check in on team progress to identify problems before they grow

---

## 5. Roles, Responsibilities, and Accountability

To keep the team organized, we may assign roles such as coordinator, note taker, repository manager, meeting facilitator, or task tracker. These roles may rotate as needed depending on workload, availability, and the needs of the project.

General responsibilities for all team members include:

- Make a good-faith effort to attend all team meetings
- Arrive on time and communicate in advance if you expect to be late or absent
- Completing assigned tasks by the agreed deadline
- Communicating promptly if a delay or issue comes up
- Contributing to team discussions, planning, and decision-making
- Reviewing shared materials when needed
- Respecting team deadlines and commitments

Accountability will be maintained through:

- Clear task assignments
- Shared knowledge of deadlines
- Regular progress updates
- Meeting notes and documented decisions
- Honest communication when expectations are not being met

---

## 6. Communication Protocols

Clear and transparent communication is essential to our success. As a team, we agree to the following communication practices:

- Use our agreed-upon communication platform for project-related messages
- Respond to important team messages within a reasonable amount of time when possible
- Notify the team as early as possible if we will be absent, late, or unable to complete a task
- Communicate blockers or confusion early instead of waiting until a deadline
- Keep communication respectful, constructive, and professional

If urgent decisions are needed, team members who are available will communicate promptly and document the outcome for the rest of the group.

---

## 7. Meeting Expectations

To make meetings productive, we agree to:

- Attend team meetings consistently and arrive on time whenever possible
- Come prepared with updates, questions, or completed work
- Stay engaged and respectful during discussions
- Keep meetings focused on agenda items and project needs
- Record important decisions, action items, and deadlines in meeting notes

If a member cannot attend a meeting, they are expected to review the notes afterward and follow up on any assigned action items.

---

## 8. Rules for Work and Collaboration

Our team rules are as follows:

- Everyone should contribute fairly to the project
- Assigned tasks should be completed on time or discussed early if delays arise
- Team members should not disappear or go silent without communication
- Important team decisions should be communicated clearly to everyone
- Work submitted on behalf of the team should reflect collective effort and appropriate review when possible
- All members should be treated respectfully, even during disagreement
- Concerns should be raised directly and professionally rather than ignored

---

## 9. Conflict Resolution

If disagreements or problems occur, we will handle them in a respectful and professional way. Our conflict resolution process will be:

1. Address the issue directly with the involved person or people in a calm and respectful way
2. If the issue is not resolved, bring it to the group for discussion
3. Focus on the behavior, process, or missed expectation rather than making personal attacks
4. Work together to identify a reasonable solution and next steps
5. If the issue continues and cannot be resolved internally, consult the mentor TA or course staff as appropriate

Our goal is to resolve problems early, fairly, and constructively.

---

## 10. If Team Rules Are Not Being Followed

If a team member is not following the agreed team rules, the team will take the following steps:

1. Give a respectful reminder of the expectation
2. Check in privately or as a group to understand whether there is an issue affecting participation
3. Clearly restate the expectations and what needs to improve
4. Document repeated issues if necessary
5. Escalate to the mentor TA or course staff if the problem continues and affects the team’s ability to function

We understand that unexpected circumstances can happen, so our first response will be communication and support. However, repeated lack of participation without communication may need to be formally addressed.

---

## 11. Measuring Success

We will measure our success by:

- Completing milestones and assignments on time
- Maintaining consistent communication
- Ensuring fair participation from team members
- Producing organized, documented, and thoughtful work
- Supporting one another and resolving issues professionally
- Creating a project that reflects both teamwork and technical effort

---

## 12. Agreement

By signing below, each team member acknowledges that they have read, understood, and agreed to follow the expectations and rules described in this team charter.
